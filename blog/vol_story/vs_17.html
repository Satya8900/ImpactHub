Published on 11 February, 2022



Story

During her course at the University, Chetona was connected with our NGO Partner - Myjis Foundation as an intern and she successfully completed role by volunteering for 350+ hours and creating an impact of Rs 18,000/- for the NGO.

Here’s what she has to share about her experience -
“Research and Community Development have always been my fields of interest, and in order to polish my skills in the mentioned fields, I joined Mayjis Foundation for an internship through ImpactHub for a period of 3 months. My role was to research and draft career guidance reports for government school students.

During my internship, I developed 12 career guidance documents, proposals and a blueprint for the execution of this program along with the timelines which will help the future volunteers at Myjis Foundation execute a similar program. The career guidance documents will help students get some guidance about their skills and help them identify the right career choice for a bright future. The experience was really nice. I could enhance my research skills and at the same time contribute towards the development of the students which I have always wished for. Mr. Ravi Shankar Denduluri, the founder of Mayjis Foundation and my mentor was really very supportive and made the internship very comfortable to pursue from my home.

For those who haven’t volunteered before, I would like to say that the experience you get by volunteering is wholesome. It not only trains you in the field of your interest, but also gives you the joy and mental peace of helping others. The exposure from these internships will help you to learn about knowing and solving real life problems. You should definitely give volunteering a shot and through my experience, I can assure that it will be worth your time!”
